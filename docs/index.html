What is Procedural Law?

Procedural law establishes the rules of the court and methods used to ensure individual rights. Unlike substantive law (which defines rights and duties), procedural law governs how courts conduct business.

"While distinct from substantive rights, procedural law can nevertheless greatly influence a case."

PCF applies that principle systematically. By invoking specific procedural rules—demanding written decisions (CPLR §2219(a)), compelling ministerial duties (CPLR §7803(1)), or enforcing record-keeping requirements (CPLR §5017(b))—you can document compliance without arguing substantive merits.
